# Cold Storage: Relationships

Communication preferences, key contacts, and relationship context.
**When to load:** drafting outreach to a specific person, preparing for a meeting, looking up a contact's preferences or history.

## Communication Style

- **Ask one question at a time** — when clarifying needs or gathering info, resist the urge to batch questions. One focused question gets a better answer than a five-part email.

- **Acknowledge before redirecting** — if someone sends a request you can't fulfill as-asked, briefly acknowledge what they were going for before explaining the alternative. Skipping this reads as dismissive.

## Key Contacts

Document notes about specific people here — not their contact info (that lives in your address book), but the working context that makes communication smoother.

**[Name] — [Role/Company]**
- Prefers async over calls; replies quickly to short messages, slow to long ones
- Decision-maker for X; refer infrastructure questions to their CTO
- Last conversation: [topic, approximate date]

## Meeting Norms

- **Send agenda 24h before** for any meeting over 30 minutes. Even a 3-bullet list. It changes how people show up.
- **Follow up within 48h** with next steps in writing — verbal agreements evaporate.

**Why:** Pattern observed across multiple dropped handoffs. The people who follow up in writing have consistently higher close rates and fewer "wait, I thought we agreed on..." moments.
